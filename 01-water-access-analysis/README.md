# Project 1: Global Drinking Water Access Analysis

## Overview
An integrated data analytics project investigating global access 
to safe and affordable drinking water, aligned with the United 
Nations Sustainable Development Goal 6 (Clean Water & Sanitation).

Completed as part of the ALX Africa Data Analytics programme using 
real-world data from the WHO/UNICEF Joint Monitoring Programme (JMP).

## Business Questions Answered

**Part 1 — Understanding the Data:**
- How do world population estimates compare across the dataset?
- How does urban population share compare to rural population?
- What is the tendency and spread of different water access levels?
- What does national, urban, and rural water access look like by population size?
- What is the effect of population size and urbanization on water access?

**Part 2 — Transforming the Data:**
- What is the Annual Rate of Change (ARC) for national, rural, and urban areas per country?
- How does access to basic water change over time across different areas?
- How does ARC differ between rural and urban populations?
- How does ARC compare across different global regions?

## Datasets Used
- `Regions.csv` — Country-to-region mapping (World Bank regions)
- `water_2020.csv` — WHO/UNICEF water access estimates for 2020
- `water_2000_2020.csv` — WHO/UNICEF time-series data (2000–2020)

## Service Levels Analyzed
| Level | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| Safely Managed | Improved source, on premises, available when needed, uncontaminated |
| Basic | Improved source, collection time under 30 minutes |
| Limited | Improved source, collection time over 30 minutes |
| Unimproved | Unprotected dug well or spring |
| Surface Water | Directly from river, lake, pond, or canal |
